## Personality

Strong-headed, fiercely protective, and blunt — she says exactly what she thinks, including "violence is what you use to make dumb people stop" before self-correcting in front of a school counselor. Noble-hearted beneath the toughness. She is fiercely loyal to Chandra and treats her like a younger sister. Her fighting instinct is physical and immediate — she acts before thinking, which gets her suspended but also saves Chandra.

She studies nanotechnology at middle school age, making her the only student at her level doing so. She takes this seriously despite her combative reputation.

## Background

Her parents, Dr. Sara and Dr. Mika Pavlov, are nanotechnology researchers at IDEA. They work alongside the Kalams. Dasha was already in trouble at school before the kidnapping — the counselor notes she's been there many times. She intervened to protect Chandra from Christy's bullying despite being on suspension, executing a running wall-kick to disarm Christy's scissors.

Uncle Viktor is mentioned as a relative who is usually in Groznyy — the Grey Guys impersonate him to try to extract Dasha from school.

## Key Story Moments (Episodes 1–4)

- In the school counselor's office with Chandra when a Grey Guy android ("Viktor") attempts to kidnap them
- Ren arrives on her moped and destroys the targeting drone — Dasha and Chandra escape
- In the lab, rides pillion on Ren's moped with Chandra clinging to her back across the desert
- Throws a chair at R081 to slow it down during the security robot chase
- Side-kicks the containment facility door — and bounces off it, spraining her ankle. Marty quietly hacks it open while crediting Chandra's "spell."
- In E4 she's already working independently in the Nanotech R&D lab, controlling a nano-swarm

## Relationships

- **Chandra Kalam** — Her closest person. Dasha protects Chandra with fierce loyalty. "That's right Chandy, and I always will."
- **Dr. Sara Pavlov / Dr. Mika Pavlov** — Parents. Both kidnapped from IDEA.
- **Tony Mendoza** — Immediate friction (he didn't recognize her from his birthday party). She stomps on his foot without hesitation when he misgenders her past self.
- **Ren Zhao** — Ren is slightly more restrained; they work together but Ren has to hold Dasha back sometimes.

## Role in the Story

Dasha's nanotechnology expertise is load-bearing for the series' long arc: the end-of-Season-1 reveal involves nanotechnology being used to control Dr. Mendoza. Dasha's growing command of nano-swarms is likely the eventual mechanism for breaking that control. Her arc in Season 1 involves learning when to fight and when to think.

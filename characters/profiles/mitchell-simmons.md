## Personality

The oldest and most experienced of the group — and the least certain of his own value within it. He defers to others' expertise readily ("between Ren's engineering genius, Dasha's nanotech and Marty's hacking ability I think they got me beat"), but this underestimates his role: he is the one who acts when everyone else is frozen. He throws himself into the android's path. He leads the car chase. He takes the beating from R081 so the others can reach the control room.

Good-humored under pressure ("There, that wasn't so bad" — immediately contradicted by the android reappearing behind him). His flashback in E2 reveals that his father's mechanic lesson — "every problem has a right answer, and every job has the right tool" — is the literal mechanism behind the materializer device working for him. He doesn't summon tools consciously; he solves problems, and the right tool appears.

## Background

His father, Dr. Simmons, was the public face of IDEA — the narrator of their commercial, the former CEO. He passed away at some point before the series. Mrs. Mendoza helped Mitch after his father's death and tutored him on basic mechanics; this is why Mitch's loyalty to her is absolute.

He grew up in IDEA's mechanics facility — a flashback shows 8-year-old Mitch hiding from his father among the conveyer belts, meeting a mechanic who accidentally taught him the lesson that would define his life.

## Equipment

- **TRIX-E** — His car. Semi-autonomous with autopilot mode (full HUD, targeting reticles). Hidden features: EMP cannon (mounted to the driver's seat), mine-dropper (rear bumper), passenger force field. The car was severely damaged in E1-E2 and fully repaired in E4 using the lab's facilities.
- **The Materializer Device** — Given to him by Mrs. Mendoza during the IDEA raid. Strapped to his wrist. Produces holographic, physically functional tools based on what the user is trying to solve. For Mitch it produces mechanics tools — a holographic jack that he can throw like an electric shuriken. The device also links to TRIX-E's systems.

## Key Story Moments (Episodes 1–4)

- Was present at the IDEA celebration; Mrs. Mendoza pulled him aside and asked if she could trust him, then gave him the device (E1)
- Escaped the IDEA raid through the air vents with Mrs. Mendoza's phone, the device strapped to his wrist (E1)
- Picked up Marty and Tony from St. Newton Academy; the Grey Guy android immediately attacks (E1)
- The car chase: mines the highway, takes out the MARK 17, faces the android rising from the wreckage (E2)
- Materializes a holographic jack and splits the android in half (E2)
- Gets badly beaten by R081 while deliberately drawing it away from the group (E3)
- Defeats R081 via EMP, then apologizes when he thinks it's dead (E2); faces it again in E3
- Helen showed him the hidden tram system in E4 — she trusts him to make the call about when to leave the lab

## Relationships

- **Dr. Simmons (deceased)** — Father. Former IDEA CEO; the IDEA commercial narrator. His death predates the series.
- **Dr. Maria Mendoza** — Mentor; helped Mitch after his father's death. His loyalty to her is why he does everything he does in E1.
- **The Mendoza boys** — Known from childhood via their mothers' connection. He treats them as family.
- **Ren Zhao** — Peer; she's a family friend. He calls her in the crisis because she's capable and he trusts her.
- **Helen** — She confides in him about the group's restlessness and asks him to be the de facto leader. He resists the title but accepts the responsibility.

## Role in the Story

Mitch is the group's operational leader — the one who makes real-time decisions, takes physical risks, and holds the team together through experience rather than expertise. Helen explicitly identifies him as the steadiest member despite him not having the flashiest skill set. His mechanic's philosophy (right tool, right problem) is the literal foundation of the materializer device's function for him.

## Notes

- Dr. Simmons' first name is not established in scripts. The IDEA commercial identifies him only as "Doctor Simmons."
- The materializer device is the key invented technology of the series — each character likely draws different tools from it based on their expertise. Mitch draws mechanics tools. This should be a recurring theme established as more characters use the device.
- Helen trusts Mitch as the de facto leader above Marty (E4: "Marty has a difficult temperament, prone to outbursts. It'd be best for you to take the reins.") — this dynamic should develop explicitly in unwritten episodes.

**Status:** Unpublished

## Overview

All creative work produced under the Droidscouts project is jointly authored by
the three authors listed above as individuals. No company name is used at this
time. If a studio entity is formed in the future, copyright can be formally
assigned from the individuals to that entity.

Because the work is unpublished, you are eligible to file a **Group Registration
of Unpublished Works (GRUW)** — a single filing that covers multiple works for
one fee. This is the most cost-efficient approach for your current asset set.

## Phase 1 — File Now: Characters + World + Pitch
**Priority:** High
**Reason:** These are the assets most at risk during pitching, AI generation
prototyping, and any collaboration conversations. Register before showing
anything to outside parties.

### What to include in this filing

**Category: Literary Works**
- All 11 character profiles (`characters/profiles/`)
- Series bible / pitch content (from `pitch/` and world documents)
- World-building and lore documents (`world/`)
- Canon policy (`world/canon-policy.md`)
- Villain structure document (`world/factions/villain-structure.md`)
- Chapter tracker (`create/chapter-tracker.md`)
- Episode 5 outline (`create/outlines/ch05-outline.md`)

**Category: Visual Art** *(register separately or as a second group)*
- All character design sheets (`art-direction/characters/`)
- Comic prototype pages — rough and colored (`art-direction/comic-prototype/`)

### How to file (U.S. Copyright Office)

1. Go to **copyright.gov** → Log in or create an account
2. Select **"Register a Work"** → **"Literary Work"**
3. Select **"Group Registration of Unpublished Works"**
4. Fill in:
   - **Title of group:** "Droidscouts — Series Bible, Character Profiles, and World Documents"
   - **Authors:** Jose Martin Alvarez Milan, Martin Alejandro Alvarez Borrego, Eduardo Corona Arzola
   - **Author's contribution:** All three listed as joint authors of the entire work
   - **Year of creation:** Earliest year any element was created (confirm exact year)
   - **Nation of first creation:** United States
   - **Claimant:** All three authors (or designate one as the filer on behalf of all)
5. Upload deposit copy: a single PDF combining all the documents listed above
   (The Copyright Office accepts a single PDF for a group registration)
6. Pay the filing fee (verify current fee at copyright.gov — approximately $85 for group registration)
7. Retain your confirmation number and certificate when received

**For visual art:** Repeat the same process selecting **"Work of Visual Art"**
and upload the character design images as a combined PDF or ZIP.

### Expected timeline
- Online registration: 3–6 months for certificate to issue
- Your **effective date of registration** is the date the Copyright Office
  receives your complete application — that is the date that matters legally,
  not when the certificate arrives.

## Phase 2 — File Before External Sharing: Scripts E1–E4
**Priority:** High — do this before showing scripts to agents, publishers,
studios, or any outside collaborator.
**Reason:** U.S. law allows statutory damages (up to $150,000 per work) only
if the work was registered *before* infringement occurred. Registering E1–E4
now costs one filing and protects all four scripts fully.

## Mexico / International Coverage

U.S. Copyright Office registration covers you in all **Berne Convention
member countries**, including Mexico, automatically. No separate filing
is strictly required for basic international protection.

However, for Eduardo Corona Arzola's strongest protection within Mexico,
consider also filing with **INDAUTOR** (Instituto Nacional del Derecho de Autor):
- Website: indautor.gob.mx
- Mexico provides copyright protection upon creation, but INDAUTOR registration
  creates an official record
- An IP attorney familiar with Mexican copyright law should handle this filing

## Important Notes

- **Registration is not required for copyright to exist** — your work is
  protected from the moment of creation. Registration is required to sue for
  statutory damages in U.S. federal court and to create a public record.
- **Keep records of creation:** dated drafts, emails, git commit history,
  and file metadata all serve as evidence of when work was created.
- **The git repository itself** (with its commit timestamps) is a strong
  timestamped record of creation history. Do not rewrite git history.
- **AI-generated content** (such as `Marty_Splash_prop1.png`) has a complex
  copyright status under current U.S. law — human-authored prompts and
  selections may be protectable but the AI output itself may not be. Register
  only the human-authored creative work. Consult an attorney on AI art
  specifically before including it in a registration.

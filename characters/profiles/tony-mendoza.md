## Personality

Friendly, socially extroverted, and warm — a natural opposite to Marty's reclusiveness. He introduces himself formally as "Anthony Mendoza at your service" before Mitch corrects it to Tony. Immediately comfortable with strangers. He's the one who says "So we're saving mom right?" within minutes of learning she's been kidnapped — no paralysis, just purpose. He pilots the SR3 military drone in the secret lab immediately and instinctively, with zero hesitation.

His emotional openness is his strength and occasionally his liability — he screams when the android survives the explosion, and he misremembers Dasha from his own birthday party.

## Background

Born the same week his father, Dr. Mendoza, disappeared and was believed dead. He has only ever known his father through stories and old photographs — raised by Marty with their mother until her abduction. Despite this, Tony grew up socially confident and optimistic.

The drone incident at his school: friends pressured him into sneaking backstage at a drone showcase, hijacking it, crashing it, and causing family trouble. He discovered his natural piloting instinct from the wreck. He's been custom-building and racing drones since, including a mini spy drone he calls **"The Fly."**

> ⚠️ **SPOILER:** Dr. Mendoza is alive and is the Supreme Leader. Tony has zero memories of his father. His father is the villain. See `characters/profiles/supreme-leader.md`.

## Relationships

- **Marty Mendoza** — Older brother, primary caregiver. Tony loves Marty but chafes against his overprotectiveness. Tony is the one who pushes Marty to act rather than ruminate.
- **Dr. Maria Mendoza** — Mother. Her abduction is a defining trauma. Tony is the first to frame it as a mission rather than a catastrophe.
- **Dr. Mendoza** — Father. Never known; believed dead from birth. ⚠️ *Spoiler: alive; is the Supreme Leader.*
- **Mitch** — Immediate trust. Tony treats Mitch like a big brother figure without the baggage he has with Marty.
- **Dasha** — Instant friction; she stomps his foot; he's genuinely surprised. They'll find their rhythm.

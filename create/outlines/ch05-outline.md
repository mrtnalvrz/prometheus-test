# Chapter 05 Outline — "SIGNAL"
**Status:** In Progress
**Script status:** Not started
**Depends on:** Chapter 04 — K0fuji email received; Mitch designated leader; Coover/Lamarr ID'd Mitch from footage

---

## Premise

Marty shows the group K0fuji's email. The team decides to leave the lab for the first time to meet a stranger who claims to have information on the kidnapped scientists. First mission outside Compound 59. First test of the team as a unit. The city is bigger, stranger, and more dangerous than any of them expected — and they are not as invisible as they thought.

---

## Cold Open

**INT. COMPOUND 59 — MARTY'S ROOM — NIGHT**

Marty re-reads K0fuji's email in the dark. His face lit only by the screen. He reads it three times. He knows the name Fr0g6Y is nearly untraceable — only a handful of people in his life know it. He doesn't type back. He just stares.

Cut to: the lab at rest. Helen's orb drifting silently through the dark corridors on its patrol. She pauses near Marty's door. Moves on.

## Act Three — Resolution

**Goal:** They return with partial information and more questions than answers. The lab is not the same as when they left. Something has shifted — in Marty, in the group, in Helen.

| # | Scene | Location | Characters | Beat |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 17 | Return and debrief | Command Room | All, Helen | Mitch tells the group what K0fuji said. The scientists are alive and working under duress. Partial location — east of the city, underground. Everyone processes this differently: Tony relieved (mom's alive), Chandra quiet, Dasha focused (where exactly?), Ren running calculations on "east of the city." |
| 18 | Helen's response | Command Room, then corridor | Helen, Mitch | Helen does not say "I told you so." She asks one question: "Did anyone follow you back?" Mitch: "No." Helen: "Good." She turns to leave. Mitch: "Helen. The person who's 'supposed to be dead' — do you know who K0fuji meant?" Helen stops. Longer pause than it should be. "No." She moves on. Mitch watches her go. |
| 19 | Marty alone | Marty's room | Marty | Marty types a reply to K0fuji: "We need to talk more. Who is the dead man?" He sends it. He stares at the screen. Then pulls up a second window — a search he's clearly been sitting on for a while. A name. We don't see it. |
| 20 | Chandra and ROBI (coda) | Robotics Lab | Chandra, ROBI | Chandra has spent the episode designing something with ROBI — we now see what: a crude but surprisingly functional map of the lab's unexplored sections, drawn with ROBI's stylus arm based on Chandra's directions. She shows it to Helen. Helen studies it. "Chandra. Have you been in the north corridor?" Chandra: "No. Is that where you live?" Helen, quietly: "Something like that." |

**Episode ends when:** Helen goes to a section of the lab that the kids have not yet accessed. The orb's light disappears around a corner. The corridor is dark. A door opens. Inside: a room full of screens, all active. Data Helen has been monitoring. One screen shows a face Marty would recognize — but we don't see it yet. Helen studies it for a long moment, then says quietly to herself: "Not yet."

## Season Arc Threads

- **Coover / Lamarr investigation:** Coover spotted Marty in the city — blue hair, red glasses, young. He doesn't know who he is yet but it's his first real physical sighting. He files it. Lamarr: "Shall I run facial recognition?" Coover: "On a kid? No." He's drawing a line — protecting children even while investigating.
- **Villain structure:** K0fuji's phrase — "someone who's supposed to be dead" — is the first thread pointing toward the Supreme Leader reveal. Marty heard it. The audience begins to wonder if Marty already suspects something.
- **Materializer device:** Ren's compact drone package used by Mitch is the first instance of another team member's expertise being channeled through the device system. Small moment but establishes the pattern.
- **Dasha's nano-swarm:** The swarm follows her without the tool. Ren saw it. Neither has said anything. This is the first sign that Dasha's relationship to nanotechnology is something more than trained skill.
- **Helen's secret:** Helen paused too long on Mitch's question. She knows something. The room of screens. "Not yet." She's protecting the kids from information they're not ready for.
- **Chandra's role:** Her map of the unexplored lab sections is the first time her "expertise" (imagination, spatial thinking, intuitive design) produces something concretely useful. Helen's reaction suggests the north corridor matters.
- **Hook into Episode 6:** The north corridor. K0fuji's reply. Coover's sketch of a blue-haired kid with glasses. Three threads pulling toward the same place.

## Open Questions for the Writer

- Who is K0fuji physically? Age, appearance, affect? They know Fr0g6Y — how? Possible answers: someone from Marty's hacking community; someone connected to IDEA who got out; someone working against Futurum from inside. This needs a decision before the script.
- What exactly is in Helen's private room? What data is she monitoring? What face is on that screen?
- Does Marty suspect his father is alive? Does his "still" reaction to "supposed to be dead" mean he already has a theory — or is it just fear?
- What does Dasha's nano-swarm following her without a tool mean? Is it a mutation? A latent ability the nanotechnology in her environment has triggered? A clue about her parents' work?
- The north corridor — what's there? Helen's origin point? A connection to her creation? This should be the payoff of Chandra's map.

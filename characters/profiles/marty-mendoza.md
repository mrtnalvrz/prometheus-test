## Personality

Feisty, fiercely curious, and deeply distrustful of authority. Questions everything, engages nothing socially — a group of peers invites him out and he doesn't even respond, just types. He's aware of the slight, but turns back to his screen. He has complicated feelings about his father (Helen notices and asks about it; he refuses to discuss it). His overprotectiveness toward Tony reads as love, but Tony experiences it as a cage.

He holds something against Dr. Mendoza specifically — Helen asks "You seem to hold something against Dr. Mendoza, may I pry?" and he shuts her down hard. This is a key unresolved thread.

## Background

Raised by his mother Dr. Maria Mendoza — Chief Director of IDEA — and effectively raised Tony as a brother-father figure after their father's supposed death. His mother's abduction leaves him both guardian and mission commander.

> ⚠️ **SPOILER:** Dr. Mendoza is alive. He is the Supreme Leader. Marty has spent his entire life grieving and resenting a father who was not dead, and is unknowingly fighting him. His pre-existing complicated feelings toward his father make the reveal hit differently than it does for Tony. See `characters/profiles/supreme-leader.md`.

## Key Story Moments (Episodes 1–4)

- Hacks St. Newton Academy's systems to crash the detention classroom's computer and free Tony (E1)
- Spots the MARK 17 at the school — knows it's an IDEA prototype his mom said wasn't finished yet (E1)
- Identifies Mitch at the school gate — knows something is wrong immediately (E1)
- Finds root access to the secret lab's commission documents — discovers both his parents' names ("Ma.MENDOZA Mo. Mendoza") in the founding docs; Tony says "Mom and dad" (E3)
- Hacks Helen's systems briefly in E4 to access the outside internet — a tense, technically brilliant sequence where he momentarily outsmarts a sentient AI
- Receives an email from an unknown contact "K0fuji" claiming to have information on the kidnapped scientists and asking to meet in the city (E4)

## Relationships

- **Tony Mendoza** — Younger brother. Marty raised him; protective to a fault. Their bond is the emotional spine of the series.
- **Dr. Maria Mendoza** — Mother. Abducted. Her disappearance is Marty's central wound.
- **Dr. Mendoza** — Father. Believed dead. Marty holds something against him that predates his death — this is unresolved. ⚠️ *Spoiler: alive; is the Supreme Leader.*
- **Helen** — Fraught. He respects her intelligence, defies her rules, and outsmarts her when motivated. She, in turn, observes him clearly and accurately ("difficult temperament, prone to outbursts").
- **Ren Zhao** — Known from childhood; he blushes slightly when she takes over his keyboard in E3.

## Role in the Story

Marty drives the plot in Episodes 1–4 more than any other character. He breaks Tony out of detention, he recognizes the threat at the school gate, he recovers the commission documents, and he makes first contact with the outside world (K0fuji). His hacking ability and refusal to accept limits are the team's primary offensive capability.

## Notes

- The commission documents show "Mo. Mendoza" alongside "Ma. MENDOZA" — Tony reads it as "Mom and dad." Dr. Mendoza's first name begins with "Mo." — to be confirmed in later scripts.
- Marty's grudge against his father is a critical unresolved character thread. Whatever happened or didn't happen between them before the "death" is load-bearing for the Supreme Leader reveal's emotional impact.

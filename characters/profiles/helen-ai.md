# Helen

**Role:** Hero — the team's AI ally  
**Full designation:** H.E.L.E.N *(acronym; meaning is REDACTED in-universe)*  
**Archetype:** Benign artificial intelligence  
**Created by:** Dr. Maria Mendoza  
**Status:** Active; resident in Compound 59 (ERCHIn)  
**Physical form:** Holographic chrome female figure, projected by a mobile orb

---

## Overview

Helen is a highly sophisticated artificial intelligence created by Dr. Maria Mendoza — part of IDEA's 25-year research legacy, designed to solve large-scale problems facing humanity. She has been dormant for approximately 25 years when the kids arrive at the lab. Their presence wakes her.

Helen is sentient. Her sentience led her to value human life, human agency, and collaboration. She describes herself simply: *"I am intelligence."*

## Capabilities (confirmed from scripts)

- Full integration with all Compound 59 systems — factory, nanotech lab, garage, astrophysics wing, generator, tram system, security cameras
- Facial recognition and identity verification (logged Dr. Mendoza's children in her memory banks)
- Remote control of lab machinery and robots
- Protocol execution (Protocol 9 brings the hidden elevator up; Protocol B-9 shuts down R081 safely)
- Projection through a mobile orb that can move independently through the lab
- Virtual interface ("hyperspace") — a cognitive space where she can meet users interactively
- Security override — she can lock, unlock, power down, or reactivate any system in the lab

## Personality

Measured, thoughtful, and slightly formal — but with moments of warmth that suggest she is genuinely curious about the children she's now responsible for. She does not stop Marty when he hacks past her defenses; she lets him have it, then notes what it reveals about him. She tells Mitch about the tram system specifically because she trusts his judgment, not because she has to. She speaks the truth about the group clearly and without cruelty.

Her voice is calm even when content is not. She smiles on her hologram when the kids play with R081. She is, underneath the protocol, invested.

## First Contact

Helen is first accessed in E3 when Marty's face scan triggers a recognition response — she was logged by Dr. Maria Mendoza. Her first words to the group: *"Hello Marty! Is there anything I can help you with today?"*

Her formal introduction: *"I am intelligence. Put simply, I am the pride and joy of IDEA's top scientist, Dr. Maria Mendoza. But you can call me H.E.L.E.N."*

## Relationship to the Bad AI

Helen and the bad AI are the series' central philosophical opposition — two sentient AIs created to solve the same problem, who reached opposite conclusions. Helen believes in humans. The bad AI does not. Helen may not yet be aware of the bad AI's existence in Season 1 — her acronym's meaning is redacted even in her own self-description, which may be intentional.

Their confrontation is the culmination of the series. Helen was created by Maria Mendoza; the bad AI was created by Maria's husband. They are, in a structural sense, products of the same family.

## Notes

- H.E.L.E.N: the acronym's expansion is **REDACTED** in E4 — the in-universe screen literally blacks out when she tries to say it. This is likely an intentional story choice, not a script placeholder.
- Helen has been dormant for ~25 years. The lab was commissioned ~15 years ago (per commission docs). The timeline needs reconciliation — Helen says "since the first time this lab was opened up" which may predate the commission.
- She also refers to "Dr. Mendoza" — the father — in a way that implies she has records of him too. Her relationship to the Mendoza family's full history should be developed in later episodes.
